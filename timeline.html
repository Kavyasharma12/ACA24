A brief look at the life of Simone Biles

  • 1997 Simone Biles was born.
  • 2003 Biles started gymnastics.
  • 2012 Biles was named to the U.S. Junior National Team.
  • August 2013 Biles was crowned the national all-around champion in USA Gymnastics National Championships.
  • October 2013 Biles competed at the 2013 World Artistic Gymnastics Championships.
  • 2013 Biles became the seventh American woman and the first African American to win the world all-around title.
  • 2014 Biles was selected to compete at the 2014 World Artistic Gymnastics Championships.
  • 2015 At the U.S. National Championships, Biles secured her third all-around national title.
  • December 2015 Biles was named Team USA Female Olympic Athlete of the Year.
  • 2016 Biles was named to the team for the 2016 Rio Summer Olympics.
  • August 2016 On August 9, Biles won her first Olympic gold medal in the gymnastics team event.
  • 2016 Simone Biles won the Glamour Award for the Record Breaker.
  • July 2017 Biles won the ESPY Award for Best Female Athlete.
  • 2017 Simone won the Shorty Awards for the best in sports.
  • 2018 In October, Biles participated in the World Team Selection Camp.
  • 2019 Biles competed at the Stuttgart World Cup, her first World Cup appearance not on American soil.
  • 2019 By winning five gold medals in Stuttgart, Biles tied the record of most gold medals won at a single World Championships.
  • 2020 At the 2020 Olympic Games, Biles performed the all-around during the qualifications.
  • February 2020 Biles was awarded the Laureus World Sports Award for Sportswoman of the Year.
  • 2021 Biles competed at the U.S. National Championships and won her 7th national all-around title.
